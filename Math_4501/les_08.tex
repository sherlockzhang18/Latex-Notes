In the Newton's Method convergence proof,
$ |x_{k+1} - \xi | \leqslant \frac{1}{2} \left|\frac{f''(\eta_{k})}{f'(x_{k})}\right| \cdot |x_{k} - \xi |^2 $. Let $ e_{k+1} = |x_{k+1} - \xi | $, $ e_{k} = |x_{k} - \xi | $.
then, 
\[
    e_{k+1} \leqslant \frac{1}{2}\left|\frac{f''(\eta_{k})}{f'(x_{k})}\right|e^2_ker
\]

Recall $ f'' $ was assumed to be continuous in a neighborhood of $ \xi  $: $ [\xi -\delta , \xi  + \delta ] $, so $ |f''(\eta_{k})| \leqslant \max|f''(x)| = M $ ($ x \in [\xi - \delta , \xi  + \delta ] $).

Because $ |f''(x)| \leqslant A \cdot |f'(y)| $ for all $ x, y \in [\xi -\delta , \xi  + \delta ]$, so $ A \cdot |f'(x_{k})| \geqslant \max_{y \in [\xi -\delta , \xi  + \delta ]} \implies |f''(x_{k})| \geqslant  \frac{m}{A}$. So $ e_{k+1} \leqslant \frac{1}{2}|M \cdot \frac{A}{M}| \cdot e_{k}^2 = \frac{1}{2} \cdot A \cdot e_{k}^2 \implies \frac{e_{k+1}}{e_{k}^2} \leqslant \frac{1}{2}A $ second convergence.
